Figure 1. The geometry of linear representations can be understood in terms of a causal inner product that respects the semantic structure of concepts. In a language model, each concept has two separate linear representations,  lambda (red) in the embedding (input context) space and  gamma (blue) in the unembedding (output word) space, as drawn on the left. The causal inner product induces a linear transformation for the representation spaces such that the transformed linear representations coincide (purple), as drawn on the right. In this unified representation space, causally separable concepts are represented by orthogonal vectors.
